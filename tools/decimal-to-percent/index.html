How to Convert a Decimal to a Percentage?

Converting a decimal to a percentage is one of the most basic yet essential mathematical operations. A decimal is simply another way of representing a fraction, and a percentage is a way of expressing that fraction as a part of 100.

To perform the conversion, you simply multiply the decimal by 100. Mathematically, this is the equivalent of shifting the decimal point two places to the right. For instance, 0.50 becomes 50%.

The Conversion Formula

Decimal × 100 = Percentage

Reverse Conversion:

Percentage ÷ 100 = Decimal

Simply multiply by 100 to go from decimal to percent, and divide by 100 to go from percent to decimal.

Common Conversion Examples

0.25 $\rightarrow$ (0.25 × 100) = 25%
0.75 $\rightarrow$ (0.75 × 100) = 75%
0.05 $\rightarrow$ (0.05 × 100) = 5%
1.2 $\rightarrow$ (1.2 × 100) = 120%
0.001 $\rightarrow$ (0.001 × 100) = 0.1%

Frequently Asked Questions

How to convert 0.75 to percent? ▾
Multiply 0.75 by 100. 0.75 × 100 = 75%. Therefore, 0.75 as a percentage is 75%.
What is 1.5 as a percentage? ▾
Multiply 1.5 by 100. 1.5 × 100 = 150%. This means the value is 150% of the original whole.
How to convert percent back to decimal? ▾
To convert a percentage back to a decimal, divide the number by 100. For example, 75% ÷ 100 = 0.75.
What is 0.05 as a percent? ▾
0.05 multiplied by 100 equals 5%.
Why multiply by 100? ▾
The word 'percent' means 'per hundred'. By multiplying a decimal by 100, you are calculating how many units out of 100 the decimal represents.
