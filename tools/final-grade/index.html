How to Calculate Your Required Final Grade?

Most courses are structured with "weighted" grades. This means some assignments, quizzes, and the final exam are worth more than others. To find your required final grade, you have to determine how much of your final grade is already "locked in" by your current performance.

By subtracting the weighted current grade from your target grade, you can isolate exactly how many points are left to be earned from the final exam alone. Our calculator does this instantly, taking into account the specific weight of your final exam.

The Final Grade Formula

Required = (Target - Current × (1 - Weight/100)) ÷ (Weight/100)

Variables Explained:

  • Target: The final percentage you want in the course.
  • Current: Your grade in the class right now.
  • Weight: The percentage the final exam contributes to the total grade.

Step-by-Step Example

Imagine you have a 75% in class, the final is worth 25%, and you want an 80% overall.

1 Find Current Weight: If the final is 25%, your current work is 75% of the grade. (0.75 × 75 = 56.25 points earned).
2 Find Needed Points: You want 80 total. (80 - 56.25 = 23.75 points needed).
3 Calculate Exam %: Divide needed points by the final weight. (23.75 ÷ 0.25 = 95%).
4 Result: You need a 95% on the final exam.

Frequently Asked Questions

What grade do I need on my final to get an A? ▾
It depends on your current grade and the final's weight. For example, if you have an 85% and the final is worth 20%, you might only need a 92% on the final to reach a 90% overall.
How is a final exam grade weighted? ▾
Weighting means the exam is worth a specific percentage of your total grade. A "20% weight" means the final accounts for 20% of your final score, while everything else combined accounts for 80%.
What if I need more than 100% on my final? ▾
If the result is over 100%, it means that even with a perfect score, you cannot reach your target grade. You may want to adjust your target or check for extra credit opportunities.
Can I use this for university courses? ▾
Yes! This calculator works for any course (High School, College, or University) as long as you know your current percentage and the weight of the final exam.
What does "exam weight" mean? ▾
It is the proportion of the total course grade that is determined by the final exam. If your syllabus says "Final Exam: 30%", the weight is 30.
