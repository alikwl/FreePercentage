How to Calculate Percentage of Marks?

Calculating your percentage is a simple way to understand your performance relative to the total possible score. Whether you are a student checking your semester results or a teacher grading papers, the process remains the same regardless of the total marks.

To get your percentage, you simply need to know how many marks you actually scored (obtained marks) and what the maximum possible score was (total marks). This tool automates the math for you, providing an instant percentage and a letter grade based on standard academic scales.

The Marks Percentage Formula

(Marks Obtained ÷ Total Marks) × 100 = Percentage

Where:

  • Marks Obtained: The actual score you achieved in the exam.
  • Total Marks: The maximum score possible for the exam or course.
  • 100: The multiplier used to convert the decimal ratio into a percentage.

Step-by-Step Example

Let's say you scored 450 marks out of a total of 600 marks.

1 Divide: Divide 450 by 600. (450 ÷ 600 = 0.75)
2 Multiply: Multiply the result by 100. (0.75 × 100 = 75)
3 Result: Your final percentage is 75%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How to calculate percentage of marks out of 600? ▾
Divide your obtained marks by 600 and multiply by 100. For example, if you got 480/600, the calculation is (480 ÷ 600) × 100 = 80%.
What is a good percentage in exams? ▾
While it varies by institution, generally 90% and above is considered excellent, 75-89% is very good, and 60-74% is considered a solid average performance.
How do I convert my percentage to a GPA? ▾
GPA scales differ by country. In the US, a 90-100% is typically an A (4.0 GPA), 80-89% is a B (3.0 GPA), and so on. Check your school's handbook for the exact conversion.
Can I calculate percentage for any total marks? ▾
Yes! This calculator works for any total marks, whether it's out of 10, 100, 500, 600, or even 10,000.
