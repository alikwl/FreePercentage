How to Calculate GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) calculation depends on whether the price provided is exclusive of tax (you need to add tax) or inclusive of tax (you need to find the base price).

When you "Add GST," you are calculating the tax on top of the net price. When you "Remove GST," you are working backwards from the final price to find out how much was the original product cost before the government's tax was added. This is essential for accounting, invoicing, and auditing business expenses.

GST Calculation Formulas

1. Adding GST (Exclusive to Inclusive):

GST Amount = (Amount × GST Rate) ÷ 100
Total Price = Amount + GST Amount

2. Removing GST (Inclusive to Exclusive):

Original Price = Amount ÷ (1 + (GST Rate ÷ 100))
GST Amount = Total Amount − Original Price

GST Calculation Examples

Example 1: Adding 18% GST to $1,000

1 GST Amount: (1000 × 18) ÷ 100 = $180.
2 Total Price: 1000 + 180 = $1,180.

Example 2: Removing 18% GST from $1,180

1 Original Price: 1180 ÷ 1.18 = $1,000.
2 GST Amount: 1180 - 1000 = $180.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GST? ▾
GST stands for Goods and Services Tax. It is a value-added tax levied on most goods and services sold for domestic consumption.
How to calculate 18% GST? ▾
Simply multiply your base amount by 0.18. For example, for $100, the GST is $18. The total including GST would be $118.
How to reverse GST? ▾
To find the amount before tax, divide the total price by (1 + tax rate). For 18%, divide by 1.18.
What are the GST slabs in India? ▾
The standard slabs are 0% (exempt), 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%.
Is GST same everywhere? ▾
No, GST rates and regulations vary by country. Always check your local tax laws for the most accurate rates.
