How to Calculate Profit Margin Percentage?

Profit margin is a financial metric used by businesses to measure the percentage of revenue that exceeds the cost of producing the goods or services sold. It is a key indicator of a company's pricing efficiency and overall financial health.

By calculating the gross profit margin, you can see how much of every dollar earned is actual profit before operating expenses. This helps business owners decide if they need to raise their prices or find ways to lower their procurement costs to maintain a sustainable business.

The Profit Margin Formulas

To fully understand your profitability, we calculate three distinct values:

Gross Profit = Revenue − Cost

The total amount of money earned after covering the cost of the product.

Profit Margin % = (Gross Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100

The percentage of the selling price that is profit.

Markup % = (Gross Profit ÷ Cost) × 100

The percentage added to the cost price to reach the selling price.

Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you buy a product for $50 (Cost) and sell it for $80 (Revenue).

1 Find Gross Profit: $80 - $50 = $30.
2 Calculate Margin: ($30 ÷ $80) × 100 = 37.5%.
3 Calculate Markup: ($30 ÷ $50) × 100 = 60%.
4 Result: Your gross profit is $30, your margin is 37.5%, and your markup is 60%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good profit margin? ▾
A good margin varies by industry. In retail, gross margins are often between 30% and 50%. In high-volume, low-margin businesses (like grocery stores), margins can be as low as 2-5%.
Difference between margin and markup? ▾
Margin is calculated based on the Selling Price, while Markup is calculated based on the Cost. If you buy for $100 and sell for $150, the markup is 50% ($50/$100), but the margin is 33.3% ($50/$150).
How to calculate net profit margin? ▾
Net profit margin considers all expenses (rent, payroll, taxes). The formula is: (Net Income ÷ Total Revenue) × 100.
What is gross profit? ▾
Gross profit is the absolute dollar amount earned after subtracting the cost of goods sold from the total revenue. It does not include indirect costs like marketing or rent.
How to improve profit margin? ▾
You can improve your margin by increasing your prices, negotiating better rates with your suppliers, or improving operational efficiency to lower production costs.
